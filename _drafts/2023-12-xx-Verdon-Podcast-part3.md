_书童按：本篇是量子计算研究者、Extropic公司创始人吉约姆·韦尔东（Guillaume Verdon）于2023年接受Lex Fridman播客采访的实录（第407集）。本篇（下篇）内容涵盖幽默与表情包文化、Jeff Bezos、Elon Musk与推特/X、Extropic公司愿景、AGI奇点、AI末日论者批判、有效利他主义批判、日常生活、身份认同、对年轻人的建议、死亡与数字永生，以及生命的意义等议题。_

幽默与表情包

Humor and memes

**Lex Fridman  (01:22:40)**： 我想问问你关于幽默和表情包的事情。@BasedBeffJezos 这个账号非常热衷于传播表情包。幽默在 e/acc 运动中扮演了什么角色？

**Lex Fridman (01:22:40)** Let me ask you about humor and memes. Like @BasedBeffJezos was very much about memes. What role does humor play in the e/acc movement?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:22:52)**： 幽默是其中的关键组成部分。我认为幽默发挥着几个重要功能。首先，它是让思想变得易于理解和令人难忘的方式。一个好的表情包能够传达复杂的思想，这是一篇论文无法做到的。它具有黏性，也易于传播。因此，幽默是传播思想的有力载体。其次，幽默是一种挑战权威和正统观念的方式，而无需表现得过于对抗。一个玩笑能说出严肃论证所无法说出的话。所以它具有这种颠覆性的品质。第三，幽默是吸引人们加入某个运动的方式。人们会被有趣、充满活力的事物所吸引。因此，如果你的运动既有趣又不拘一格，它吸引的人就会比那种沉闷严肃的运动多得多。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:22:52)** Humor is a key part of it. And I think humor serves several functions. First, it's a way to make ideas accessible and memorable. A good meme can communicate a complex idea in a way that a treatise cannot. It's sticky and it's spreadable. And so humor is a powerful vector for idea propagation. Second, humor is a way to challenge authority and orthodoxy without being overtly confrontational. A joke can say something that a serious argument cannot. And so it has this subversive quality. And third, humor is a way to attract people to a movement. People are drawn to things that are fun and joyful. And so if your movement is funny and irreverent, it attracts more people than if it's dour and serious.

**Lex Fridman  (01:23:41)**： 这里是否存在一种风险，就是幽默可能会削弱那些严肃的思想？

**Lex Fridman (01:23:41)** Is there a risk that the humor can undermine the serious ideas?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:23:46)**： 确实如此。我认为存在这样的风险：这个运动因为幽默而被认为不够严肃。我自己也经历过这种情况。有些人不认真对待这些思想，因为它们是以表情包的形式呈现的。所以，幽默所带来的可及性与严肃所带来的可信度之间，存在一种张力。但我的观点是，一个好的思想应该能够经受住被制作成表情包的考验。如果它经受不住，也许它本身就算不上什么好思想。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:23:46)** Yes. I think there's a risk that the movement gets dismissed as not serious because of the humor. And I've experienced that myself. Some people don't take the ideas seriously because they're packaged in memes. And so there's a tension between the accessibility that humor provides and the credibility that seriousness provides. But my view is that a good idea should be able to withstand being put in a meme. And if it can't, maybe it's not that good an idea.

**Lex Fridman  (01:24:19)**： 也就是说，思想本身要足够强大，才能在表情包的形式下存活下来。

**Lex Fridman (01:24:19)** Like the ideas should be strong enough to survive the meme form.

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:24:23)**： 没错。表情包的形式本身就是对思想的一种检验。因为如果你无法将一个思想表达成一个表情包，也许它太过复杂，或者也许它并没有你以为的那么深刻。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:24:23)** Right. And the meme form is a test of the ideas. Because if you can't express an idea as a meme, maybe it's too complicated or maybe it's not as insightful as you thought.

Jeff Bezos

Jeff Bezos

**Lex Fridman  (01:24:41)**： 我们来聊聊 Jeff Bezos。你之前提到你很欣赏 Jeff Bezos。你欣赏他什么？

**Lex Fridman (01:24:41)** Let's talk about Jeff Bezos. You mentioned that you admire Jeff Bezos. What is it that you admire about him?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:24:48)**： 我认为 Jeff Bezos 是我们这个时代最具影响力的建造者之一。他从零开始将亚马逊打造成全球最大的零售商和最强大的科技公司之一。他创建了 Blue Origin，追求人类成为星际文明的愿景。而他之所以能做到这一切，在于他对长远目标的持续专注。他以"第一天"哲学著称，这一哲学要求你始终把自己当作公司的第一天来行事。永远不要自满，永远不要让官僚主义和惰性侵蚀进来。我发现这深深地激励着我，因为这正是 e/acc 哲学的体现。这是对达到平衡状态的拒绝，是对永续增长与更新的承诺。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:24:48)** I think Jeff Bezos is one of the most consequential builders of our time. He built Amazon from nothing to the largest retailer in the world and one of the most powerful technology companies. He built Blue Origin to pursue his vision of humanity becoming a space-faring civilization. And he's done this by being relentlessly focused on the long term. He's known for his Day One philosophy, which says that you should always act as if it's Day One of the company. Never become complacent. Never let bureaucracy and inertia set in. And I find that deeply inspiring because it's a manifestation of the e/acc philosophy. It's the refusal to be at equilibrium. It's the commitment to perpetual growth and renewal.

**Lex Fridman  (01:25:40)**： 你怎么看 Blue Origin 以及整体的太空探索？

**Lex Fridman (01:25:40)** What's your take on Blue Origin and space exploration generally?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:25:46)**： 我认为太空探索是人类能够做的最重要的事情之一。而私营部门的参与，通过 SpaceX、Blue Origin 以及其他公司，已经带来了革命性的改变。它大幅降低了进入太空的成本，并开启了一些在太空完全由政府主导时代不可能实现的可能性。我认为，从长远来看，成为一个多星球物种对于人类的长期生存至关重要。所以我深度支持太空探索，也支持 Jeff Bezos 和 Elon Musk 所展现出的雄心壮志。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:25:46)** I think space exploration is one of the most important things humanity can do. And I think the private sector's involvement, through SpaceX and Blue Origin and others, has been transformative. It's reduced the cost of getting to space dramatically. And it's opened up possibilities that weren't possible when space was exclusively the domain of governments. And I think in the long run, becoming a multi-planetary species is critical for the long-term survival of humanity. And so I'm deeply in favor of space exploration and of the ambition that Jeff Bezos and Elon Musk are showing.

**Lex Fridman  (01:26:23)**： 这与 e/acc 的联系真的很有趣。向太空的扩张，是生命与智慧扩张的另一种形式。

**Lex Fridman (01:26:23)** That's a really interesting connection to e/acc. The expansion into space as another form of the expansion of life and intelligence.

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:26:32)**： 确实如此。可以说，这是在扩展生命之火的燃烧范围。它将生命这一热力学过程的触角延伸到新的领域。所以从 e/acc 的视角来看，太空探索与这一运动的哲学是高度契合的。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:26:32)** Exactly. It's expanding the range of the fire of life, if you will. It's extending the reach of the thermodynamic process that is life into new territory. And so from the e/acc perspective, space exploration is deeply consistent with the movement's philosophy.

**Lex Fridman  (01:26:50)**： 你曾经以 Beff Jezos 的身份与 Jeff Bezos 交谈，而现在你以 Beff Jezos 背后真实的人的身份，坐在这里与 Lex Fridman 对话。你觉得 Jeff Bezos 会怎么看待 e/acc 和 Beff Jezos 这个账号？

**Lex Fridman (01:26:50)** You spoke to Jeff Bezos, or Beff Jezos you spoke to, and now you're speaking as Beff Jezos, the man behind the Beff Jezos account, to Lex Fridman. What do you think Jeff Bezos would think of e/acc and of Beff Jezos?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:27:06)**： 我想他大概会欣赏其中的精神。他并不以善于玩表情包著称，但我认为其背后的哲学与他所体现的那种着眼长远、雄心优先的思维方式是相符的。他的股东信谈到了长远、谈到了发明、谈到了游走探索新领域的重要性。这些都与 e/acc 的哲学高度一致。所以我认为他会理解，即便他不一定会称自己为 e/acc 的支持者。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:27:06)** I think he would probably appreciate the spirit of it. He's not known for being a meme lord, but I think the underlying philosophy resonates with the kind of long-term, ambition-first thinking that he embodies. His shareholder letters talk about the long-term, about invention, about the importance of wandering and exploring new territory. And these are very consistent with the e/acc philosophy. So I think he would get it, even if he wouldn't necessarily call himself e/acc.

Elon Musk

Elon Musk

**Lex Fridman  (01:27:42)**： 你怎么看 Elon Musk？

**Lex Fridman (01:27:42)** What do you think about Elon Musk?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:27:45)**： 我认为 Elon Musk 是当今在世的最非凡的人类之一。我认为他之所以非凡，在于他将远见卓识、强大的执行力与承担巨大风险的意愿融于一身。当 Tesla 和 SpaceX 陷入困境时，他押上了自己的全部身家。这种冒险精神极为罕见。大多数拥有他那样资源的人会选择分散投资、稳中求进。但他却选择加倍押注，而如今这两家公司都成为了各自领域的领导者。这深深地激励着我。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:27:45)** I think Elon Musk is one of the most extraordinary humans alive. And I think what makes him extraordinary is his combination of vision, execution, and willingness to take enormous risks. He bet his entire fortune on Tesla and SpaceX when they were failing. And that kind of risk-taking is very rare. Most people with his resources would have diversified and played it safe. And instead he doubled down and now both companies are the leaders in their respective fields. And I find that deeply inspiring.

**Lex Fridman  (01:28:24)**： 那 Twitter，也就是现在的 X 呢？

**Lex Fridman (01:28:24)** And Twitter, now X?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:28:26)**： 我认为收购 Twitter 是一个大胆的举动。我认为其背后的意图与他所宣称的保护言论自由的目标是一致的。长远来看这是否能够成功，目前还是个未知数。我认为 Twitter 作为公共话语的平台具有重要意义。而将它交到一个致力于保护言论自由的人手中，比交给那些更倾向于压制言论的人要好。但执行过程颇为坎坷。所以我对结果抱有希望，但并不确定。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:28:26)** I think the acquisition of Twitter was a bold move. And I think the intent behind it is consistent with his stated goal of protecting free speech. Whether it will succeed in doing that in the long run is an open question. I think Twitter as a platform for public discourse is important. And I think having it in the hands of someone who is committed to free speech is better than having it in the hands of people who are more inclined to suppress speech. But the execution has been rocky. And so I'm hopeful but uncertain about the outcome.

**Lex Fridman  (01:29:04)**： 你如何看待言论自由与真相之间的关系？因为在 Twitter 或 X 这样的平台上，很多言论内容并不真实。

**Lex Fridman (01:29:04)** How do you think about the relationship between free speech and truth? Because sometimes a lot of the speech on platforms like Twitter or X is not true.

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:29:14)**： 是的。我认为这是言论自由争论中最深层的张力之一。我的观点是，解决方案不是压制虚假言论，而是鼓励真实言论，并建立能够浮现和奖励真相的系统。因为压制虚假言论的问题在于：谁来决定什么是虚假的？历史上，那些拥有权力宣判言论为虚假的机构，往往都滥用了这种权力。所以我认为解决方案在于需求端。教导人们去评估证据，成为批判性思考者，抵抗操纵。而不是在供给端，阻止某些言论被说出口。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:29:14)** Yeah. I think this is one of the deep tensions in the free speech debate. And my view is that the solution is not to suppress false speech, but to encourage true speech and to build systems that surface and reward the truth. Because the problem with suppressing false speech is who decides what's false? And historically, the entities that have had the power to declare speech false have often abused that power. And so I think the solution is on the demand side. Teach people to evaluate evidence, to be critical thinkers, to resist manipulation. Rather than on the supply side, preventing certain speech from being said.

**Lex Fridman  (01:30:01)**： 我很喜欢这个框架。这是一个非常优雅的表述。真相的需求端与供给端。

**Lex Fridman (01:30:01)** I like that. That's a very elegant framing. Demand side versus supply side of truth.

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:30:07)**： 这里有一个市场的类比。在市场中，你不会阻止人们生产劣质产品，而是教育消费者去追求优质产品。劣质产品因为无人购买而自然消亡。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:30:07)** And there's a market analogy. In markets, you don't prevent people from making bad products. You educate consumers to demand good products. And the bad products disappear because people don't buy them.

Extropic

Extropic

**Lex Fridman  (01:30:22)**： 我想问问关于 Extropic 的事情。你们在解决什么问题？公司目前处于什么阶段？

**Lex Fridman (01:30:22)** Let me ask about Extropic. So what problem are you solving and what is the status of the company?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:30:28)**： Extropic 正在解决 AI 计算中的能源效率问题。当前 AI 计算的主流范式是数字计算，也就是 GPU，它们非常耗电。随着 AI 模型越来越大，对电力的需求也在急剧增长。人们越来越担忧，我们正在逼近数字计算在合理能耗预算内所能承受的极限。因此，Extropic 正在构建模拟热力学计算硬件，让计算直接在硬件的物理特性中完成，从而驾驭热力学过程来实现计算。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:30:28)** So Extropic is solving the problem of energy efficiency in AI compute. The current paradigm for AI compute is digital compute, GPUs, and they're very power hungry. And as AI models get bigger and bigger, the power requirements grow enormously. And there's a growing concern that we're hitting the limits of what digital compute can do within reasonable power budgets. And so Extropic is building analog thermodynamic compute, hardware that harnesses thermodynamic processes to do the compute natively in the physics of the hardware.

**Lex Fridman  (01:31:05)**： 目前进展如何？你们现在在制造硬件吗？

**Lex Fridman (01:31:05)** What's the status? Are you building hardware right now?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:31:08)**： 是的，我们正在制造第一批芯片。我们仍然是一家处于相对早期阶段的公司。我们已经完成了融资，拥有一支由才华横溢的物理学家和工程师组成的团队。我们正在进行第一批芯片的流片和测试工作，希望今年能够验证这一概念。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:31:08)** Yes, we're in the process of building our first chips. We're still a relatively early stage company. We've raised funding and we have a team of talented physicists and engineers. And we're in the process of taping out our first chips and testing them. And we're hopeful that we'll be able to demonstrate the concept this year.

**Lex Fridman  (01:31:31)**： 目标应用场景是什么？

**Lex Fridman (01:31:31)** What's the target application?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:31:33)**： 目标应用场景是生成式 AI。包括扩散模型、基于能量的模型，以及任何需要从概率分布中采样的模型。我们针对的具体指标是能源效率和延迟。我们希望能以远低于当前数字方法所需的能耗和时间来生成样本。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:31:33)** The target application is generative AI. So diffusion models, energy-based models, any model that requires sampling from a probability distribution. And the specific metrics we're targeting are energy efficiency and latency. We want to be able to generate samples much faster and with much less power than current digital approaches.

**Lex Fridman  (01:31:57)**： 这种高效率背后的物理原理是什么？

**Lex Fridman (01:31:57)** What's the physics behind the efficiency?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:32:00)**： 核心洞见在于，数字计算机被设计为确定性的。它们与热噪声对抗。每一个晶体管都被设计为处于确定的状态，零或一，任何可能将其推向中间状态的热涨落都被视为错误并加以抑制。而这种抑制是有代价的，它耗费能量。但如果你反过来，设计一种专门利用随机性的硬件，拥抱热涨落而非对抗它，你就不再需要支付这种抑制代价。热涨落会为你完成采样的工作。这样，你就能从热力学中免费获得计算工作。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:32:00)** So the key insight is that digital computers are designed to be deterministic. They fight against thermal noise. Every transistor is designed to be in a definite state, zero or one, and any thermal fluctuations that might push it to an intermediate state are treated as errors and suppressed. And that suppression has a cost. It costs energy. But if you instead design hardware that is designed to be stochastic, that embraces the thermal fluctuations, you no longer have to pay that suppression cost. And the thermal fluctuations do the work of sampling for you. And so you get computational work for free from the thermodynamics.

**Lex Fridman  (01:32:47)**： 这有点像是把自然界的随机性当作一种资源来利用。

**Lex Fridman (01:32:47)** It's kind of using the randomness of nature as a resource.

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:32:51)**： 确实如此。正如生命利用自由能作为资源一样，我们正在将热随机性作为一种计算资源加以利用。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:32:51)** Exactly. Just as life uses free energy as a resource, we're using thermal randomness as a computational resource.

**Lex Fridman  (01:32:59)**： 你对 Extropic 未来五到十年的愿景是什么？

**Lex Fridman (01:32:59)** What's your vision for Extropic in five to ten years?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:33:03)**： 我希望我们能够验证这一概念，并扩展到构建数据中心规模的热力学计算。我希望这能成为 AI 计算的新范式，比当前的数字范式更加节能。从长远来看，我希望这能够催生一种与物理世界更紧密结合的 AI，由环境热力学过程提供动力，并因此更加节能，也因此更加普及。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:33:03)** I hope that we'll have proven the concept and that we'll be scaling up to build data center scale thermodynamic compute. And I hope that this becomes a new paradigm for AI compute that is much more energy efficient than the current digital paradigm. And in the long run, I hope that this enables a kind of AI that is much more tightly integrated with the physical world, that is powered by ambient thermodynamic processes, and that is much more energy efficient and therefore much more pervasive.

**Lex Fridman  (01:33:39)**： 这与生物计算有关联吗？因为生物大脑本身也是热力学计算系统。

**Lex Fridman (01:33:39)** Is there a connection to biological computing? Because biological brains are also thermodynamic computing systems.

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:33:46)**： 非常有关联。大脑是一种利用热力学过程的模拟计算系统。神经元是随机性装置，它们以概率方式放电。大脑的计算是数十亿个这样的随机装置相互作用后涌现出的属性。从这个意义上说，大脑已经在做 Extropic 正在努力人工实现的事情——它在利用物质的热力学属性来计算。所以是的，这里有深刻的生物学类比。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:33:46)** Yes, very much so. The brain is an analog computing system that harnesses thermodynamic processes. Neurons are stochastic devices. They fire probabilistically. And the brain's computation is an emergent property of billions of these stochastic devices interacting. And in that sense, the brain is already doing what Extropic is trying to do artificially. It's harnessing the thermodynamic properties of matter to compute. And so yes, there's a deep biological analogy.

**Lex Fridman  (01:34:23)**： 你是否希望在某个未来，计算机与生物体之间的界限会变得模糊？

**Lex Fridman (01:34:23)** Is your hope that in some future world, the line between computer and biology becomes blurred?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:34:30)**： 是的。我认为从长远来看，生物智能与人工智能之间的区别将会消融。我们将拥有一个从纯生物到混合再到纯人工的智能连续谱。而所有这些都将建立在相同的热力学原理之上。所以从某种意义上说，生命之火将会扩展，将那些建立在相同物理学基础上的人工智能形式也纳入其中。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:34:30)** Yes. I think in the long run, the distinction between biological and artificial intelligence will dissolve. And we'll have a spectrum of intelligence from purely biological to hybrid to purely artificial. And all of it will be built on the same thermodynamic principles. And so in some sense, the fire of life will expand to include artificial forms of intelligence that are built on the same physics.

奇点与 AGI

Singularity and AGI

**Lex Fridman  (01:35:02)**： 我想问问你关于奇点和 AGI 的看法。你相信技术奇点这个概念吗？

**Lex Fridman (01:35:02)** Let me ask you about the singularity and AGI. Do you believe in the concept of a technological singularity?

有效利他主义

Effective altruism

**Lex Fridman  (01:39:46)**： 我想问问你关于有效利他主义的看法。e/acc 与 EA 是什么关系？

**Lex Fridman (01:39:46)** Let me ask you about effective altruism. How does e/acc relate to EA?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:39:53)**： e/acc 与 EA 之间的关系颇为复杂。它们有一些共同关注的领域，尤其是围绕生存风险和人类的长远未来。但它们有着截然不同的哲学基础。EA 植根于功利主义伦理学，以及我们能够也应该计算出能做到的最大善，然后付诸实践这一理念。而 e/acc 则植根于物理学，以及增长与扩张本身具有内在价值这一理念——你不需要计算最优结果，你只需要维持允许增长和探索的条件即可。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:39:53)** So e/acc and EA have a complicated relationship. They have some overlapping concerns, particularly around existential risk and the long-term future of humanity. But they have very different philosophical foundations. EA is rooted in utilitarian ethics and in the idea that we can and should calculate the most good we can do and then do it. And e/acc is rooted in physics and in the idea that growth and expansion are intrinsically valuable, that you don't need to calculate the optimal outcome, you just need to maintain the conditions that allow growth and exploration.

**Lex Fridman  (01:40:31)**： 你认为 EA 的主要错误是什么？

**Lex Fridman (01:40:31)** What do you think are the main mistakes of EA?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:40:35)**： 我认为主要的错误在于假设你能够计算出最优结果。功利主义要求你将所有个体的福祉加总成一个数字，然后最大化这个数字。但这是一个极其困难的计算，而且历史上这种计算已经被用来为许多糟糕的事情辩护。每当你说目的证明手段合理时，你就是在进行功利主义式的推理。问题在于，这种计算往往是错的。所以，为你所计算出的最优结果而优化，在你的世界模型有误的情况下，可能会导致灾难性的后果。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:40:35)** I think the main mistake is the assumption that you can calculate the optimal outcome. Utilitarianism requires you to aggregate the well-being of all individuals into a single number and then maximize that number. But this is an extremely hard calculation, and it's a calculation that has been used to justify some very bad things historically. Any time you say the ends justify the means, you're doing utilitarian reasoning. And the problem is that the calculation is often wrong. And so optimizing for your calculated optimal outcome can lead to catastrophic outcomes if your model of the world is wrong.

**Lex Fridman  (01:41:16)**： e/acc 是如何避免这一错误的？

**Lex Fridman (01:41:16)** And how does e/acc avoid that mistake?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:41:19)**： e/acc 的方式是根本不去尝试计算最优结果。与其试图推算出最好的未来然后将其实现，e/acc 说的是：维持允许探索和适应的条件。保持高度的多样性，保持强有力的竞争，让系统自己找到最优路径。这本质上就是市场的运作方式，也是进化的运作方式。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:41:19)** E/acc avoids it by not trying to calculate the optimal outcome. Instead of trying to calculate the best future and then bring it about, e/acc says, maintain the conditions that allow exploration and adaptation. Keep the variance high. Keep competition strong. And let the system find its own optimal path. Which is essentially what markets do and what evolution does.

**Lex Fridman  (01:41:48)**： 你认为 Sam Bankman-Fried 事件对 EA 最重要的教训是什么？

**Lex Fridman (01:41:48)** What do you think is the most important lesson from the Sam Bankman-Fried situation for EA?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:41:55)**： 我认为这一教训是，将后果主义推理发挥到极致，可以为任何行为辩护。如果你足够坚定地相信自己在做最大的善，你就能说服自己欺诈是合理的，背叛他人的信任是合理的。我认为这是 EA 深层次的失败模式。当个人将后果主义推理推向其逻辑终点时，他们可能会得出极度不道德的结论。我认为 EA 需要认真审视这一失败模式。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:41:55)** I think the lesson is that consequentialist reasoning taken to an extreme can justify any action. If you believe strongly enough that you're doing the most good, you can convince yourself that fraud is justified, that betraying the trust of others is justified. And I think that's a deep failure mode of EA. When individuals take consequentialist reasoning to its logical conclusion, they can arrive at conclusions that are deeply unethical. And I think EA needs to grapple seriously with that failure mode.

**Lex Fridman  (01:42:30)**： 你认为这是个人的问题，还是哲学本身的问题？

**Lex Fridman (01:42:30)** Do you think it's a problem with the individuals or with the philosophy?

**Guillaume Verdon  (01:42:34)**： 我认为问题出在哲学本身。我认为这一哲学对个人判断过于宽容。它给予后果计算过多的分量，而给予规则和约束的分量不足。我认为解决方案是增加更多的义务论约束——无论后果如何都绝不违反的规则。这些规则能够对更极端形式的后果主义推理形成制衡。

**Guillaume Verdon (01:42:34)** I think it's with the philosophy. I think the philosophy is too permissive of individual judgment. It gives too much weight to the calculation of consequences and not enough weight to rules and constraints. And I think the solution is to add more deontological constraints. Rules that you don't violate regardless of the consequences. And those rules provide a check on the more extreme forms of consequentialist reasoning.
